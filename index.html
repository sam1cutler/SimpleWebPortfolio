About me

I graduated from Amherst College with a degree in Biology. After college, I spent a year teaching English in China, then returned to the U.S., working for two years in Boston, MA as a research technician studying the immune response to HIV infection.

I then moved to Seattle, where I earned a PhD in molecular and cellular biology at the University of Washington, with my doctoral research focused on how DNA is packaged and organized in cells. While working conducting this research, I learned some Python, Bash, and R, and found that I loved programming.

After completing my PhD, I worked as an undergraduate biology lecturer, and I am now working to transition to a career in software development.
