About me

Since graduating from Amherst College with a degree in biology, I have...

  1. taught high school English in Hunan Province, China
  2. worked as a research technician in Boston, MA, studying the immune response to HIV infection
  3. earned a PhD in molecular and cellular biology at the University of Washington, with doctoral research focused on how DNA is packaged and organized in cells
  4. taught undergraduate biology classes at the University of Washington and Edmonds Community College

While conducting my PhD research, I used a lot of Python, Bash, and R, and found that I loved programming. In particular, I found it immensely gratifying to use R to create beautiful, publication-quality figures that looked just how I wanted; or to write Python scripts that could efficiently analyze huge, genome-wide data sets; or to create Bash scripts that could transform three hours of painstaking, repetitive typing at the command-line into a simple process of "run one script, go have lunch, and come back to folders full of neatly analyzed data"! Based largely on that experience, I am now extremely excited to be pursuing a career in software development.

Outside of my interests in programming/science/teaching, I love to spend as much time as possible in the outdoors, whether rock climbing, hiking, backpacking, biking, or snowboarding.
