About me

Since graduating from Amherst College with a degree in biology, I have...

  • taught high school English in rural Hunan Province, China
  • worked as a research technician in Boston, MA, studying the immune response to HIV infection
  • earned a PhD in molecular and cellular biology at the University of Washington, with my doctoral research focused on how DNA is packaged and organized in cells
  • taught undergraduate biology classes at the University of Washington and Edmonds Community College

While conducting my PhD research, I used a lot of Python, Bash, and R, and found that I loved programming. In particular, I found it immenseley gratifying to write Python scripts that could efficiently analyze huge genome-wide data sets, or to make Bash scripts that could transform three hours of painstaking command-line typing into a single "run the script, go have lunch, and come back to folders full of neatly analyzed data"! Based on that experience, I am now excited to be transitioning to a career in software development.

Outside of my interests in programming/science/teaching, I love to spend as much time as possible in the outdoors, whether rock climbing, hiking, backpacking, biking, or snowboarding.
